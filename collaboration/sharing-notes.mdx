Sharing a note in PeerNotes is how a private idea becomes a team resource. Whether you're publishing a finished summary, a work-in-progress you want feedback on, or a quick update for the whole team, sharing moves your thinking from your personal space into the workspace where everyone can engage with it.

## How to publish a note to the shared workspace

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the note you want to share">
    Navigate to your personal notes and open the one you'd like to publish. You can share any note — drafts, finished pieces, or everything in between.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a topic">
    Before publishing, assign the note to a relevant topic. This places it in the right section of the shared workspace so teammates can find it alongside related notes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your visibility setting">
    Decide whether to share the note with your whole team or with specific teammates only. See the next section for a full breakdown of your options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish">
    Hit **Publish to workspace**. Your note is now live in the shared workspace — teammates with access will be notified and can start reading, listening, or contributing right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Choosing who sees a note

PeerNotes gives you control over who can view each note you share:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Team-wide" icon="globe" href="/collaboration/shared-workspace">
    Publish the note to the full shared workspace. Every member of your team can see, read, listen to, and contribute to it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Selective sharing" icon="user-group" href="/collaboration/sharing-notes">
    Share the note with specific teammates only. Useful for early-stage ideas, sensitive topics, or drafts you want reviewed before broader publication.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  You can change a note's visibility after publishing. Promote a selectively shared note to team-wide when it's ready, or restrict a published note if the conversation needs to move to a smaller group.
</Info>

## How teammates are notified

When you publish a note, PeerNotes automatically notifies the people who should see it:

- **Team-wide notes** trigger a notification for every workspace member, visible in their notification feed and the workspace **Recent** section.
- **Selectively shared notes** send a notification only to the specific teammates you've chosen — keeping inboxes clean and attention focused.

Teammates can adjust their notification preferences in their account settings if they want to tune how often they're alerted to new shared notes.

## Listening vs. reading

Not every teammate will read your shared note the same way. PeerNotes supports both reading and listening, so teammates can engage with your thinking in whatever way works best for them.

<Card title="Listen to shared notes" icon="headphones" href="/guides/listen-to-workspace">
  Teammates can listen to any shared note as audio — great for catching up on new thinking during a commute, between meetings, or whenever reading isn't practical. Learn how listening works in the workspace guide.
</Card>

## Tips for writing notes that are easy to share

A well-prepared note is easier for teammates to read, engage with, and build on. Keep these habits in mind before you hit publish:

<Tip>
  **Lead with the point.** Start your note with the key takeaway or conclusion, not the backstory. Teammates who are skimming will get the most important thing immediately, and those who want detail can keep reading.
</Tip>

- **Use clear section headings** to break up longer notes — teammates can jump to the section most relevant to them.
- **Link to related notes** already in the workspace so readers have context without you having to repeat it.
- **State what kind of input you want.** If you're sharing a draft for feedback, say so at the top. If the note is a finished record, make that clear too — it sets the right expectations for how teammates should engage.
- **Keep it focused.** One note, one core idea. If you're covering multiple distinct topics, consider splitting them into separate notes and linking them together.

<Warning>
  Avoid publishing incomplete notes without a clear label. If a note is a work-in-progress, add a short note at the top — for example, "Draft — feedback welcome." This prevents teammates from treating an unfinished idea as a final decision.
</Warning>
