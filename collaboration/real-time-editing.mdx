Real-time collaboration in PeerNotes means your thinking never has to wait for a meeting. When you publish a note to the shared workspace, your teammates can jump in immediately — adding context, pushing back on assumptions, asking questions, and building the idea further. Every contribution appears live, so the note evolves in front of everyone together.

## How to collaborate on a note

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a shared note">
    Navigate to the shared workspace and open any published note. You'll see the note content alongside a live activity panel showing who else is viewing or contributing right now.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your contribution">
    Select any part of the note to leave a comment, add context, or suggest an edit. Your contribution is attached directly to the relevant section — keeping feedback specific and easy to follow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch teammates contribute live">
    As other teammates add comments or edits, you'll see them appear in real time without refreshing. The note fills in as a conversation, not just a static document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolve and build">
    Once a thread of comments has been addressed or folded into the note, mark it resolved to keep the view clean. The note grows sharper with every round of contribution.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What teammates can contribute

Collaboration in PeerNotes isn't limited to simple edits. Teammates can engage with a note in several ways:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Comments" icon="comment" href="/collaboration/real-time-editing">
    Leave feedback on any section of a note — reactions, suggestions, or follow-up thoughts tied directly to the relevant text.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Context" icon="circle-info" href="/collaboration/shared-workspace">
    Add background information that enriches the note without changing its core argument — useful for linking related research or prior decisions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Questions" icon="circle-question" href="/guides/writing-notes">
    Flag assumptions or gaps by posing a question directly in the note. Questions prompt the author and other readers to think more carefully.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Edits" icon="pen-to-square" href="/guides/writing-notes">
    Suggest or make direct edits to the note content — refining wording, adding missing detail, or tightening the argument with the author's permission.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How collaboration makes thinking sharper

A note written alone reflects one perspective. A note that teammates have engaged with reflects many. When someone adds context you didn't have, spots a weak assumption, or asks the question you forgot to answer, the idea gets stress-tested in real time — and the final note is far stronger for it.

PeerNotes is designed around this principle: **thinking together produces better outcomes than thinking in parallel.** The workspace isn't a place to store finished ideas; it's a place to develop them with the people who matter.

<Note>
  All contributions — comments, edits, and added context — are visible to every teammate with access to the note as soon as they're made. There's no "draft comment" state; once you submit, it's live.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Bring your AI assistant into the mix alongside your teammates. While your team stress-tests an idea from a human perspective, use the [AI assistant](/guides/ai-assistant) to pressure-test assumptions, explore counterarguments, or summarize the discussion so far — giving your collaboration an extra layer of rigor.
</Tip>
