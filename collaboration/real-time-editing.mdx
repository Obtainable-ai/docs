A note doesn't have to be a solo effort. Add teammates as **collaborators** and a note becomes shared work: they can open it, read the current draft, and contribute their own thinking. Collaboration in PeerNotes is deliberate rather than chaotic — you choose who's in, and the note shows exactly who's involved.

## Add collaborators to a note

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the note">
    Collaboration is per-note: you invite people into a specific piece of thinking, not your whole library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add teammates">
    Open the note's sharing controls and add collaborators from your workspace. Each collaborator gets access to read and contribute to the note.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work the draft together">
    Collaborators see the latest version whenever they open the note. The note lists its collaborators — <i>"With alex@…, sam@…"</i> — so everyone knows who's shaping the idea.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What collaboration is for

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pressure-testing" icon="circle-question">
    Invite the teammate most likely to disagree. If the argument survives their pass, it's ready for the whole workspace.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Filling gaps" icon="puzzle-piece">
    Different people hold different pieces. A note about a customer problem gets stronger when support, product, and engineering can each add what they know.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Co-authoring" icon="pen-to-square">
    Some notes are joint work from the start — a proposal two of you are drafting, a decision record for a shared project.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Review before publishing" icon="badge-check">
    Use collaborators as your editorial pass before a note is [published as an article](/core-concepts/articles) for everyone.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Keep the collaborator list small while the idea is fragile. Two or three engaged readers produce better feedback than a dozen passive ones — you can always publish to everyone when it's ready.
</Tip>

## Collaboration and the rest of the pipeline

Only **notes** are collaborative. Your [thoughts](/core-concepts/thoughts) and [sources](/core-concepts/sources) stay private to you, and [articles](/core-concepts/articles) are read-only for the workspace — updated by republishing the note behind them. That separation keeps the messy early thinking private and the collaborative stage explicit.
