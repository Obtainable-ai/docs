A **workspace** is where a group thinks together in PeerNotes — a seminar, a research group, a knowledge team, a book club. Each member captures privately inside it; what the group shares is the developed thinking. One home, two layers: yours, and the team's.

## What's yours, what's shared

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Private to each member" icon="lock">
    Your [thoughts](/core-concepts/thoughts) and [sources](/core-concepts/sources) — the raw, unfiltered capture layer. No teammate ever sees these unless you develop them into something shared.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Shared with the workspace" icon="users">
    [Notes](/core-concepts/notes) you've opened to teammates, and [articles](/core-concepts/articles) published for everyone — organized by the [topics](/core-concepts/topics) the workspace thinks about.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

This split is what makes PeerNotes work: capture stays honest because it's private, and the shared layer stays high-signal because everything in it was deliberately shared.

## Members and administrators

Workspaces have two roles:

- **Members** capture their own thoughts, collaborate on notes they're invited to, and read everything published to the workspace.
- **Administrators** manage the workspace itself — its settings, members, and topics.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a workspace">
    Give it a name, and optionally seed it with the topics you know you'll be thinking about.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite members by email">
    Add teammates from workspace settings — you can invite members and add topics anytime.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add administrators (optional)">
    Share the admin role with co-organizers who should manage members and settings alongside you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Navigate the workspace

- **Sidebar feeds** — Thoughts, Notes, Sources, and Articles each have their own feed, so you always know which layer you're looking at.
- **Search** — press **⌘K** (Mac) or **Ctrl K** (Windows) to search across your workspace from anywhere.
- **Topics** — browse the Topics page to see the workspace's themes and dive into everything on a subject.
- **Chat** — ask the [AI assistant](/guides/ai-assistant) about anything in the workspace.

## Multiple workspaces

You can belong to several workspaces — say, one for your team and one for a reading group — and switch between them from the workspace switcher. Each workspace is fully separate: its own members, topics, content, and chat.

<Tip>
  **Treat articles as the workspace's record.** When thinking matures — a decision, a finding, a synthesis worth keeping — publish it. Over time the Articles feed becomes the knowledge base a new member can read to understand how the group thinks.
</Tip>
