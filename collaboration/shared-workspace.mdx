The shared workspace is the beating heart of PeerNotes. Instead of ideas scattered across inboxes, chat threads, and private documents, your team's notes, topics, and thinking all live in one organized place — so nothing gets lost and everyone always knows where to look.

## What lives in the shared workspace

Your shared workspace brings together everything your team publishes and builds together:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Notes" icon="file-lines" href="/guides/writing-notes">
    Published notes from any teammate — meeting takeaways, research summaries, working drafts, and finished thinking.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Topics" icon="folder-open" href="/collaboration/shared-workspace">
    Organized categories that group related notes, so your team can find everything on a subject in one place.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Clips" icon="scissors" href="/guides/web-clipper">
    Saved highlights and snippets your team has pulled from notes or external sources for quick reference.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Team thinking" icon="users" href="/collaboration/real-time-editing">
    Comments, questions, and context that teammates have added to notes — the live, evolving layer on top of every idea.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to navigate the workspace

Finding what you need in a growing workspace is fast when you know where to look.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse by topic">
    Use the topic list in the left sidebar to jump straight to a subject area. Topics act like organized folders — select one to see every note published under it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search across all notes">
    Press **⌘ K** (Mac) or **Ctrl K** (Windows) to open search. Type any keyword, phrase, or teammate's name to surface relevant notes instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check recent notes">
    The **Recent** feed on the workspace home surface the latest published notes from your team — a quick way to stay current without hunting around.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Keeping the workspace organized

A clean workspace makes it easier for everyone to contribute and find what they need. A few simple habits go a long way:

- **Assign every note to a topic** before publishing it. Unorganized notes are hard to rediscover later.
- **Use clear, descriptive titles** — write titles that explain the note's point, not just its subject. "Q3 pricing research summary" is more useful than "Pricing notes."
- **Archive stale notes** rather than deleting them. Archived notes stay searchable but don't clutter the main workspace view.
- **Review topics regularly** as a team and merge or rename any that overlap — keeping the topic list tight makes navigation intuitive for everyone.

## Who can see what

<Info>
  By default, notes published to the shared workspace are visible to every member of your team. When you publish a note, it becomes part of your team's shared record immediately.
</Info>

If you want to share a note with only specific teammates before publishing broadly, you can share it selectively. See [Sharing Notes](/collaboration/sharing-notes) for a full walkthrough of visibility options.

<Tip>
  **Use the shared workspace as your team's single source of truth.** When a decision is made, a project wraps up, or an important idea emerges, publish it to the workspace. Over time, this builds a living knowledge base your whole team can draw on — reducing repeated questions and making onboarding new teammates much faster.
</Tip>
