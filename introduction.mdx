PeerNotes is a workspace for people who read a lot and want to retain more of it. Most note apps help you _store_ ideas — but storage isn't understanding. Real comprehension comes from reacting to what you read, connecting those reactions over time, and explaining your thinking to peers who push back. PeerNotes is built around exactly that loop.

## The pipeline: from reading to shared knowledge

Everything in PeerNotes flows through four layers. Each one has its own page in **Core Concepts** — start here for the shape of the whole:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Sources" icon="box-archive" href="/core-concepts/sources">
    What you read. Upload PDFs, documents, images, and videos, or clip web pages with the browser extension. Private raw material.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Thoughts" icon="lightbulb" href="/core-concepts/thoughts">
    Your reactions, captured in seconds. Private to you, and automatically clustered into topics by the PeerNotes agent — no filing.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Notes" icon="file-lines" href="/core-concepts/notes">
    Thoughts compiled into structured thinking. Share a note with workspace members for review and challenge — like code review, for ideas.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Articles" icon="newspaper" href="/core-concepts/articles">
    Refined notes, published to the whole workspace. The moment private thinking becomes shared knowledge.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Running through all four are [Topics](/core-concepts/topics) — themes the agent discovers in your thinking and uses to keep everything organized — and the [AI assistant](/guides/ai-assistant), a thinking partner you can ask questions from anywhere in your workspace.

## How it works day to day

<Steps>
  <Step title="Capture as you go">
    Clip the article you're reading, upload the report you were sent, and jot thoughts the moment they occur — from the web app, the browser extension, or the mobile apps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let the agent organize">
    The PeerNotes agent clusters your thoughts into topics continuously. When you come back, your scattered fragments are already grouped into the themes you've been thinking about.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compile and refine">
    When a topic ripens, select its best thoughts and convert them into a note. Structure the idea, attach your sources, and ask the assistant what's missing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share and publish">
    Open the note to teammates for feedback. When it holds up, publish it as an article for the whole workspace to read.
  </Step>
</Steps>
