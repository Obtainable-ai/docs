Some of your best thinking happens away from a keyboard — mid-walk, mid-commute, or in the middle of a conversation. PeerNotes dictation lets you speak an idea out loud and captures it as a note the moment you're done, so nothing slips away before you have a chance to write it down properly.

## When to use dictation

Dictation isn't just for when your hands are full. It works especially well in situations where typing slows you down or breaks your flow:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="On the go" icon="person-walking" href="/guides/dictate-a-note">
    Capture ideas during a commute, a walk, or any moment away from your desk. Speak naturally and review the transcript when you're back.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Brainstorming" icon="lightbulb" href="/guides/dictate-a-note">
    Talk through a problem out loud without worrying about structure or spelling. Get every thread of an idea down fast, then shape it into a note later.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Rough drafts" icon="pencil" href="/guides/dictate-a-note">
    Dictate a first pass at something before you write it. Speaking your thinking often surfaces angles you wouldn't have found staring at a blank page.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to dictate a note

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open PeerNotes">
    Go to [peernotes.io](https://peernotes.io) and open your workspace. You can dictate from the web app on any device with a microphone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the dictate button">
    Look for the microphone icon in the toolbar or on the new note screen. Tap it to start a dictation session. PeerNotes will ask for microphone permission the first time you use this feature — allow it to continue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak your idea">
    Talk naturally. PeerNotes transcribes your speech in real time, so you can watch the words appear as you speak. Pause at natural breaks — the transcription catches up quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your transcript">
    When you've finished speaking, tap the microphone icon again to stop recording. Read through the transcript and make any quick edits — fix misheard words, add punctuation, or trim anything you want to leave out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the note">
    Give your note a title and tap **Save**. Your dictated note is added to your workspace, ready to develop further or share with your team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for better dictation

A few small habits make a big difference in transcription accuracy:

<Tip>
  Speak at a steady, natural pace. Rushing tends to run words together and introduces more errors than speaking a little slower than you normally would.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Find a quieter environment when you can. Background noise — traffic, music, crowded spaces — can trip up transcription, especially for technical terms or names.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Always do a quick review before saving. Transcription is accurate but not perfect. A 30-second scan catches the few misheard words that would otherwise make your note confusing later.
</Tip>

<Info>
  You can dictate as long or as short a note as you need — a single sentence or several minutes of thinking. There's no time limit on a dictation session.
</Info>

## Where dictated notes appear

Dictated notes land in your workspace just like any note you type by hand. They appear in your workspace feed, are visible to your teammates in the shared workspace, and can be organized with topics the same way as any other note. There's nothing that marks a note as "dictated" once it's saved — it's just a note.
