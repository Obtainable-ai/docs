Writing in PeerNotes usually doesn't start with a blank page. It starts with the [thoughts](/core-concepts/thoughts) you've already captured — and a note is where you compile them into something structured. This guide walks through the full writing flow, from selecting thoughts to publishing an article.

## Start from your thoughts (recommended)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review a topic">
    Open the Thoughts feed and filter by the topic you want to write about — or browse the Topics page to see where thinking has accumulated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the thoughts that hold up">
    Select each thought you want to include. A floating bar tracks your selection at the bottom of the screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Convert to Note">
    Choose **Convert to Note**. Your selected thoughts land in a new note, ready to be shaped into prose.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can also start a blank note from the Notes feed when you already know what you want to say.

## Write and structure

The editor is built for prose: headings, bullet and numbered lists, bold and italic, and links. Everything saves automatically as you type — close the tab mid-sentence and your draft will be waiting.

A few practices that make notes your peers can actually engage with:

<Tip>
  **Lead with your point.** Put the conclusion, question, or recommendation in the first sentence. Teammates skimming the feed decide from that line whether to open the note.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **One idea per note.** Focused notes are easier to discuss, easier to find, and easier to publish. If you're covering two things, write two notes.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Attach your sources.** When a note cites the material it came from, reviewers can check the original rather than taking your word for it. See [Sources](/core-concepts/sources).
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Ask the assistant before you share.** The [AI assistant](/guides/ai-assistant) will tell you what a careful reader is going to ask. Better to hear it before your teammates do.
</Tip>

## Tag topics

Add [topics](/core-concepts/topics) to keep the note discoverable alongside the related thoughts and articles in your workspace. Thoughts you compiled usually carry their topics with them.

## Share, then publish

Notes start private. When a draft is ready for other eyes:

1. **Share with workspace members** — open the note's share panel and choose who can read and respond. This is the feedback stage: teammates challenge the argument, fill gaps, and push the thinking further.
2. **Publish as an article** — when the note holds up, select **Publish**. The publish dialog previews the article (title, excerpt, reading time) and lets you choose whether the working note stays visible. Published [articles](/core-concepts/articles) reach every workspace member.

<Info>
  Keep editing after publishing — you can **republish** at any time and the article updates in place.
</Info>
