Not every moment of catching up happens at a desk. PeerNotes lets you listen to your workspace — your team's notes, ideas, and developing thinking — as audio, so you can stay across what's happening even when you're away from a screen. It's the same content you'd read in the workspace, delivered in a format that fits around the rest of your day.

## When listening works well

Listening is designed for the in-between moments — times when you want to stay connected to your team's thinking without carving out dedicated screen time:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Commutes" icon="train-subway">
    Turn a daily commute into catch-up time. Listen to recent notes from your team and arrive at work already across the key ideas of the day.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Walks and exercise" icon="person-walking">
    Keep your body moving and your mind engaged. A 20-minute walk is enough to get through several notes and stay connected to your team's work.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Between meetings" icon="calendar">
    Use a short gap between meetings to listen to a specific note before you discuss it. Arrive with context instead of skimming on the way in.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to start listening

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your workspace">
    Go to [peernotes.io](https://peernotes.io) and sign in. Your workspace feed shows all the notes your team has published.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select what you want to hear">
    Choose a single note to listen to, or start a listening feed to hear a sequence of recent notes from your workspace. You can filter by topic to focus on a specific area of your team's work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Press play">
    Tap the **Listen** button on the note or feed you've selected. PeerNotes converts the content to audio and begins playing immediately. You can use your device's standard playback controls — pause, skip back 15 seconds, or move to the next note.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep listening in the background">
    PeerNotes audio continues playing when you lock your screen or switch to another app, so you can listen without keeping the app open.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you can listen to

Listening works across the content in your shared workspace:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Individual notes" icon="file-lines">
    Open any note and tap **Listen** to hear it read aloud. Useful when you want to hear a specific piece of thinking before a meeting or discussion.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Workspace feed" icon="rss">
    Listen to a curated sequence of recent notes from your whole workspace — a catch-up mode that gives you a broad picture of what your team has been working on.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Topic feeds" icon="tag">
    Filter by topic to hear only the notes related to a particular project or area. Useful for focused catch-up when you've been away from a specific workstream.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Clipped articles" icon="newspaper">
    Web clips saved to the workspace can also be listened to — so articles and references your teammates saved are part of the listening experience too.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  Listening is available on any device with a speaker or headphones — desktop, mobile, and tablet. No separate app is required.
</Info>

## Tips for building a listening habit

<Tip>
  **Connect listening to an existing routine.** The easiest way to make listening stick is to pair it with something you already do — a morning commute, an afternoon walk, or a post-lunch stretch. Attach it to the routine rather than deciding each time.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use topic filters for focused catch-up.** If you've been out for a few days or away from a particular project, filter the listening feed to that topic first. You'll get a concentrated catch-up instead of a broad sweep of everything.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Listen before you write.** Hearing what your teammates have been thinking about before you sit down to write your own note often surfaces connections you wouldn't have found otherwise. A 10-minute listen can make a note significantly stronger.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Listening is designed for catching up, not replacing active reading. For notes that need your close attention — decisions, detailed proposals, technical specs — read them properly when you're at your desk.
</Warning>
