The PeerNotes AI assistant is a thinking partner built into your workspace. Open a chat, ask it anything, and it responds with your workspace as context — your thoughts, notes, topics, and sources. It's designed around a simple belief: the best way to help you think is to ask better questions, not to hand you someone else's words.

## Start a conversation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Chat">
    Select **Chat** in the sidebar. Your previous conversations are listed on the left, so you can pick up an earlier thread or start fresh.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask your assistant">
    Type into the **Ask your assistant…** box. Ask about your own thinking ("What have I been capturing about onboarding lately?"), a source you added, or a draft you're developing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep the thread">
    Each conversation is saved per workspace. Long-running threads — a project, a book you're working through — can live as their own conversations you return to over time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## A thinking partner, not a ghostwriter

Most AI writing tools offer to write things for you. The PeerNotes assistant takes the opposite approach: the goal isn't to produce text, it's to improve _yours_.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Critical questions" icon="circle-question">
    Ask the assistant to read a draft note and it surfaces the questions a careful reader would ask — the ones you need to answer before publishing.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Assumption spotting" icon="magnifying-glass">
    It flags claims doing a lot of work without much support, so you can decide whether to back them up or rethink them.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Workspace recall" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    The assistant knows what's in your workspace. Use it to resurface thoughts you captured weeks ago that bear on what you're writing now.
  </Card>

  <Card title="On-demand, not intrusive" icon="comment">
    You invite the assistant in when you want it. It doesn't monitor your writing or offer unsolicited feedback.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  The assistant is most useful on notes where you have a real point to make but aren't sure it holds up yet. Bring it a draft you'd want a thoughtful colleague to read before you share it with the team.
</Tip>

## The agent behind the scenes

Chat is the conversation you start; the PeerNotes agent also works continuously in the background:

- **Organizing** — clustering your new thoughts into [topics](/core-concepts/topics) as you capture them
- **Connecting** — linking related thinking across your workspace so themes surface on their own

The result is a workspace that gets more organized the more you use it — without you doing the organizing.

<Info>
  The assistant works within one workspace at a time. Switch workspaces and your chat follows, with that workspace's content as context.
</Info>
