The PeerNotes web clipper lets you save anything you find online — an article, a page, a passage, or a highlighted quote — directly into your workspace without breaking your reading flow. Every clip lands with its source URL attached, so you always know where an idea came from when you're ready to develop it further.

## What you can clip

You can capture more than just full pages. The web clipper supports several clip types to match whatever caught your attention:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Full articles" icon="newspaper" href="/guides/web-clipper">
    Save the entire readable content of an article or blog post, stripped of ads and navigation clutter.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Full pages" icon="browser" href="/guides/web-clipper">
    Capture a complete web page — useful for reference pages, documentation, or anything you want to preserve as-is.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Highlighted text" icon="highlighter" href="/guides/web-clipper">
    Select any passage on a page and clip just that excerpt. The surrounding context and source URL are saved alongside it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Quotes" icon="quote-left" href="/guides/web-clipper">
    Clip a specific quote from an article or page to use as a starting point for a note or discussion in your workspace.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to clip a page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the PeerNotes browser extension">
    If you haven't already, install the extension from your browser's extension store. See [Install the browser extension](/getting-started/install-extension) for instructions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse to the page you want to save">
    Navigate to the article, page, or content you want to clip. If you want to clip a specific passage, highlight it before opening the extension.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the PeerNotes extension icon">
    Find the PeerNotes icon in your browser toolbar and click it to open the clipper panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your clip type">
    Select whether you want to save the full article, the full page, or your highlighted selection. A preview of the content appears in the panel so you can confirm what will be saved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save to your workspace">
    Click **Save to workspace**. The clip is added to your PeerNotes workspace instantly, with the source URL attached.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How clips appear in your workspace

Once saved, your clip appears in your workspace as a clip item — distinct from a note so you can tell at a glance what's raw material and what's developed thinking. Each clip shows:

- The **title** of the page or article it came from
- The **source URL**, so you can navigate back to the original at any time
- The **clipped content** — the full article, page text, or your selected excerpt
- The **date and time** it was saved

Your teammates can see clips in the shared workspace too, making them a great way to share interesting reading with your team without having to paste links into a chat.

## Turn a clip into a note

Clips are raw material. When you're ready to build on something you've saved, you can develop it into a full note right from the clip view.

<Tip>
  Open a clip and select **Develop into note** to create a new note pre-populated with the clipped content and its source. From there, add your own thinking, structure the idea, and share it with your team.
</Tip>

<Note>
  The web clipper requires the PeerNotes browser extension. Visit [Install the browser extension](/getting-started/install-extension) to get set up.
</Note>
